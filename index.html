How it works

A base station: the "home" for the mower. The mower automatically docks and recharges here. A perimeter fence: a "dog fence" wire is installed that defines the mowers area of operation.

A mower: a battery powered mower, managed by state of the art sensors and onboard computer.

Frequently Asked Questions

What about Team members relation to the introduction of autonomous mowing?
Properties require still visits to maintain the property.
Lawn areas under care of robotic mowers still require periodic visits by team members to monitor operation and perform maintenance on the units.
In a commercial setting: Team members are instructed as to the positive environmental benefits of adopting autonomous mowing as part of a program that uses less environmentally negative impactful property maintenance equipment and how that contributes to the environmental efforts for and with our customers.
Safety
Each mower used in the Eigenmow program has sensors that shutoff mower if it is picked up.
Each mower used in the Eigenmow program uses sensors that redirect mower upon contact with an obstacle.
A user defined PIN number can be set so that mowers menu can only be accessed after entering it. The PIN number cannot be changed – even if the battery is removed or the unit is reset – to reduce the possibility of theft.
While each unit has been designed with comprehensive safety features, each unit is an active lawn mower and should be respected as such.
Pets/wildlife
Dogs:The mowers operate with a perimeter fence similar to an underground dog fence. Does this impact dogs with the collar? “There has never been a reported impact of the dog collar being activated by the fence.” Also they operate different frequencies.
No negative impact to wildlife has been seen. The rabbits and squirrels observed watching the mowers seemed wary but amused around the mower
Does the mower operate in the rain?
Each unit mower used in the Eigenmow program have built in adjustable rain sensors that instruct the mower to cease mowing and return to the base station. Mowing will then resume at the next programmed period, catching up on the lost mowing time later in the week.
Can I name the mower
Of course
The winning name at the office was ‘mowbot’, although, in all honesty, I voted for naming the mower ‘lulu’.
Can I have a striped ‘Baseball field’ pattern in my lawn?
No. The mowers operate by mowing in a random pattern in the areas they maintain.
